ABOUT US

Anabel Egner and Philipp Eckel met while studying architecture at KIT Karlsruhe, Germany. After five years working in renowned offices in Berlin, they decided to move to Santiago de Chile in 2024.
Here they founded PHILYBEL - a collaborative practice that explores the production of architecture through multiple approaches, always seeking reinvention and refusing to be confined by style or dogma.

Playful yet strict. Green but never washed. Elegant but raw.
Talk less, make more.


PHILIPP ECKEL, M.Sc. Arch. (KIT Karlsruhe), completed his Master’s thesis with Prof. M. Frohn and Prof. G. Vrachliotis on film theory and its application to architectural design strategies. From 2018 to 2023, he worked on diverse conversion and reuse projects at Huetten und Palaeste in Berlin. During this time, he contributed to projects that received the German Sustainability Award 2024, were finalists in the DAM Prize and featured in publications such as Arch+ 256.
In 2024, he co-founded FHAO.
Philipp is a registered architect with the Architektenkammer Berlin and currently teaches a design studio at UDLA, Santiago.

ANABEL EGNER, M.Sc. Arch. (KIT Karlsruhe), completed her Master’s thesis under Prof. Meinrad Morger and Prof. Riklef Rambow. She has worked on residential and public buildings with a focus on educational facilities and participated in various competitions, including ZWT - Med Campus Graz (1st Prize) and Bildungs- & Integrationszentrum Buch (2nd Prize) for Riegler Riewe Architekten, as well as Museo Rapa Nui (Honourable Mention) for Max Nunez.
