ABOUT US

Anabel Egner (she/her) and Philipp Eckel (he/him) met while studying at KIT Karlsruhe. After five years working in renowned offices in Berlin, they decided to move to Santiago de Chile. As employees, they contributed to projects that received the German Sustainability Award 2024, were finalists for the DAM Preis, and were published in various journals including Arch+.
In 2024, they founded PHILYBEL — a collaborative practice that explores the production of architecture through multiple approaches, always seeking reinvention and refusing to be confined by style or dogma.

Playful yet strict. Sustainable but never greenwashed. Every line is worth something. Slick but dirty. Talk less, make more. Every project could be the last — so make it count.


PHILIPP ECKEL, M.Sc. Arch. (KIT Karlsruhe), completed his Master’s thesis with Prof. M. Frohn and Prof. G. Vrachliotis on film theory and its application to architectural design strategies. He worked on diverse conversion and reuse projects at Huetten und Palaeste in Berlin (2018–2023) and co-founded FHAO (Office for Future, Hope and Optimism) in 2023. Philipp is a registered architect with the Architektenkammer Berlin and currently teaches a design studio at UDLA, Santiago.

ANABEL EGNER, M.Sc. Arch. (KIT Karlsruhe), completed her Master’s thesis under Prof. Meinrad Morger and Prof. Riklef Rambow. She has worked on residential and public buildings with a focus on educational facilities and participated in various competitions, including ZWT (1st Prize) and BIZ (2nd Prize) for Riegler Riewe Architekten, as well as Museo Rapa Nui (Honourable Mention) for Max Nunez.
