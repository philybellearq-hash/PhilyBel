ABOUT US

Anabel Egner (she/her) and Philipp Eckel (he/him) met while studying at KIT Karlsruhe. After five years working in renowned offices in Berlin, they decided to move to Santiago de Chile. There, they founded PHILYBEL, a collaborative practice that explores the production of architecture through diverse approaches — always seeking reinvention and refusing to be limited by style or dogma.
Playful yet precise. Sustainable but never greenwashed. Every line matters. Slick yet raw. Talk less, make more. Every project could be the last — so make it count.
PHILIPP ECKEL, Philipp Eckel M.Sc., registered architect at the Architektenkammer Berlin, studied at KIT Karlsruhe and briefly at UPC Barcelona, where he developed a passion for cinema. This led to his 2018 master’s thesis at KIT with Prof. Marc Frohn and Georg Vrachliotis, exploring film theory as a tool for architectural design strategies. From 2018 to 2023, he worked at Hütten und Paläste, contributing to numerous reuse and conversion projects, including U-Halle Mannheim – Phase 1 (BUGA), awarded the Deutscher Nachhaltigkeitspreis 2024, finalist for the DAM Preis 2024, and featured in Arch+ and other publications. In 2024, he co-founded FHAO (Office for Future, Hope and Optimism), which is currently completing the second phase of the U-Halle Mannheim industrial conversion. His broad interest in architecture lies within ecosistemic thinking, the development of design strategies and space.
